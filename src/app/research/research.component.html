Insights From Our Study

1. In a world of big data, visual cues matter

We found that participants take much longer to navigate to raw numbers than to targets on a visualization that uses years, dates, and minutes.


V.S.

2. Timelines should be vertical, not horizontal

For the traditional zooming methods such as two-finger pinch zoom and scroll zoom, participants of the study navigated faster using a vertical timeline than a horizontal timeline on mobile, and equal on desktop.

For more advanced methods that have user-controlled zoom speed, such as brush or dynamic zoom, the vertical orientation led to faster results than horizontal, and the difference is much bigger than for the traditional methods.
